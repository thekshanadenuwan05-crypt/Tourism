About Anuradhapura

The sacred ancient capital that shaped the soul of Sri Lanka for over 1,300 years.

Ancient Kingdom of Glory

Founded in the 4th century BCE by King Pandukabhaya, Anuradhapura was the center of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization for over 1,300 years. It was the island's first true capital, a city made of grand palaces, monasteries, bathing pools, and magnificent stupas that rivaled the ancient capitals of India and Rome.

When a sapling from the Bodhi Tree in Bodh Gaya was brought to Sri Lanka in the 3rd century BCE, Anuradhapura became the spiritual center of the island. The Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi, planted by Princess Sangamitta, still grows here today — the oldest recorded tree planted by human hands.

Popular Places

Explore the most sacred and magnificent sites of the ancient kingdom.

Sacred

Sri Maha Bodhi

The oldest documented tree on earth, a direct descendant of the tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment. Planted over 2,300 years ago by Princess Sangamitta.

Learn More
Iconic

Ruwanwelisaya

Built by King Dutugemunu in 140 BC, this magnificent white stupa rises 103 meters high. Guarded by 344 elephant statues, it enshrines the most Buddha relics in the world.

Learn More
Historic

Jetavanaramaya

Once the tallest brick structure in the ancient world at 122 meters. Built in the 3rd century CE by King Mahasena, this ochre-hued stupa dominated the skyline for centuries.

Learn More
Ancient

Isurumuniya Temple

Built into rock in the 3rd century BC, this temple features the famous Isurumuniya Lovers carving and the oldest visible pagoda in the world. A masterpiece of rock architecture.

Learn More
Monastic

Abhayagiri Stupa

The centerpiece of a 5,000-monk monastery, this dagoba originally stood 100 meters high. A major center of Buddhist learning that attracted scholars from across Asia.

Learn More
Pilgrimage

Mihintale

The cradle of Buddhism in Sri Lanka. Where Arahath Mahinda Thero preached Buddhism to King Devanampiyatissa in 236 BC. Climb 1,840 steps for panoramic views.

Culture & Food

Experience the vibrant traditions, festivals, and culinary delights of Sri Lanka.

Sri Lankan Traditions

Ayurveda, meditation, and the timeless practice of Buddhism shape daily life. Experience the warm hospitality, traditional dance forms like Kandyan and Sabaragamuwa, and the reverence for elders that defines our culture.

Vesak Festival

The Festival of Lights celebrates Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and passing. Streets glow with lanterns, pandals display Jataka tales, and dansalas offer free food to all. The entire island becomes a sparkling wonderland.

Poson Festival

Commemorating the arrival of Buddhism in 236 BC. Pilgrims climb Mihintale in white, carrying lanterns and offerings. The mountain comes alive with devotional songs, sermons, and the spirit of faith.
